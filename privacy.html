Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 2026

The short version: ContactFixer does not collect, store, transmit, or share any of your data. Your contacts stay on your device. There are no servers, no accounts, and no analytics.

Information we collect

None. ContactFixer does not collect any personal information, usage data, crash reports, or analytics of any kind.

How your contacts are used

ContactFixer requests access to your contacts solely to perform its core function: reading phone numbers and writing standardized versions back to your device. Specifically, the app:

  • Reads contact names and phone numbers to identify numbers missing the +1 country code
  • Writes updated phone numbers back to the same contacts using Apple's native Contacts framework
  • Does not read any other contact fields (email, address, notes, etc.)
  • Does not store, cache, or log any contact information
  • Does not transmit any contact data over the network

All processing happens entirely on your device. No contact data ever leaves your iPhone.

Network access

ContactFixer makes no network requests of any kind. The app has no backend, no API, and no external services. You can verify this by running the app in airplane mode — it works identically.

Third-party services

ContactFixer uses no third-party SDKs, analytics tools, advertising networks, or crash reporting services. The only framework used is Apple's native Contacts framework, which is part of iOS.

Data retention

Because no data is collected, there is nothing to retain or delete. Uninstalling the app removes it entirely from your device with no residual data anywhere.

Children's privacy

ContactFixer does not collect information from anyone, including children under the age of 13.

Changes to this policy

If this privacy policy changes, the updated version will be posted on this page with a revised date. Given the nature of the app — no data collection — meaningful changes to this policy are unlikely.
